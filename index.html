Case Overview
Karnataka State CID · Filed: 15 October 1987

Deceased: Raghavendra Narasimha Iyer, 58 years. Founder of Iyer Silk Emporium, one of Mysore's most prestigious silk trading houses. Known as a shrewd businessman and a difficult man to please.

Found: 7:14 AM, 15 October 1987, in his private study on the upper floor of Iyer Haveli, #12 Sayyaji Rao Road, Mysore. Discovered by his personal valet, Krishnamurthy.

Cause of Death: Suspected poisoning. Post-mortem confirmed acute arsenic toxicity. Death estimated between 10 PM and 1 AM on the night of 14th October.

Original Ruling: Suicide — based on a contested note found at the scene. Case closed November 1987. The ruling was disputed by the eldest daughter and now, fresh evidence has prompted this reinvestigation.

Context: The victim had recently revised his will — disinheriting his eldest son Vikram and leaving the majority of the ₹40 lakh estate to a charitable trust. Three family members and two business associates were present in the haveli that night.

The Iyer Family
Background & Relations

Raghavendra Iyer built his silk empire from a small loom in T. Narasipur. He married Savithri in 1955; she passed in 1979. Together they had three children: Vikram (eldest son, 34), Malathi (daughter, 30), and Suresh (youngest son, 26).

In the months before his death, Raghavendra had grown increasingly suspicious of financial irregularities at his emporium — specifically targeting Vikram, who managed the accounts. He had consulted his lawyer, Advocate Gopalakrishnan, just three days before his death.

Also present that night: Dr. Sujatha Reddy, Raghavendra's personal physician who made a house call earlier that evening, and Bhaskar Rao, a business rival turned associate.

Evidence Sealed
Crime Scene Report
Iyer Haveli · Upper Study · October 15, 1987

The study is a large room approximately 20×15 feet, lined with teak bookshelves and a central writing desk. One window overlooking the inner courtyard was found unlatched — unusual, as the deceased was known to keep it bolted after dark.

Raghavendra was found slumped in his high-backed chair, right arm hanging limp. A half-consumed glass of warm milk sat on the desk, along with scattered papers and an open ledger. No signs of physical struggle were observed.

A handwritten note was found tucked beneath the desk blotter, reading: "I have betrayed those who trusted me. There is no other way. — R.N.I." — however, the handwriting has since been flagged as inconsistent with authenticated samples from the deceased.

Scene Observations
Items of Interest

🥛 Glass of milk — sent for toxicology. Arsenic compounds confirmed. The milk was prepared in the kitchen by the cook and brought up at 9:30 PM per routine. However, the cook states it was delivered to a different room initially.

📖 Open ledger — accounts for October 1987. Several entries circled in red ink, suggesting the deceased had been auditing the books that night. Pages 88–91 are missing.

🔒 Locked drawer — the bottom-right desk drawer was locked. When forced open during investigation, it contained a separate will dated October 10, 1987 — newer than the will filed with Advocate Gopalakrishnan.

🧤 Residue — a faint white powdery residue was found inside the rim of a teaspoon near the milk glass. Only partially tested in 1987.

🪟 Unlatched window — the window leads to a narrow balcony accessible via the rear staircase, which does not pass through the main hall. Anyone using it could enter or exit without being seen.

Witness Statements
Recorded October 15–18, 1987 · Original transcripts
Statement
Muniamma
Cook · 22 years service · Age 48

"I prepared the milk at nine-thirty as always. I put it on the tray myself. Krishnamurthy anna took it up. I went to my room and was sleeping by ten. Then — maybe at half past eleven, I hear footsteps. Heavy ones. On the back stairs. I thought it was Suresh anna coming back from outside. He sometimes uses the back way when he comes home late."

⚠️ Key detail: Muniamma confirms the milk was placed directly on the tray by her and taken by Krishnamurthy. No one else handled it before delivery — unless it was tampered with en route or in the study itself.

Statement
Rangaiah
Gate Watchman · Age 61

"Doctor madam came at seven-forty and left at... I wrote it down. Eight-twenty. No, wait — there was a problem with the register that night. The page got wet in the rain. I am not able to say exactly. But I think she left. I was not always at my post — I went to use the outhouse for some time."

⚠️ Key detail: The watchman cannot confirm Dr. Reddy's departure time. His register entry is illegible. This creates a window in which she could have remained on the property until well after 10 PM.

Statement
Advocate Gopalakrishnan
Family Lawyer · Age 56

"Raghavendra came to me on the 11th. He was very clear. He wanted to change the will — to leave the emporium to a charitable trust and provide small allowances only. He said Vikram had been stealing from him and he had proof. He planned to confront Vikram that weekend. He was not suicidal — he was angry. Very angry."

⚠️ Key detail: Raghavendra had proof of embezzlement and intended to confront Vikram. The missing ledger pages 88–91 almost certainly contained this proof. Their disappearance is central to the case.

Statement · 2024 (New)
Bhaskar Rao
Business Associate · Now 79 · Interviewed 2024

"I visited that night because Raghavendra had accused me of colluding with Vikram to move inventory without his knowledge. I was furious — I did nothing wrong. I told him so. We argued, yes. But I left at nine-fifteen. On my life, I left. And I can tell you — when I was in that sitting room, I saw Vikram in the hallway, on a telephone call, whispering. That is unusual — there was no phone in the hallway. He must have brought the handset from somewhere."

⚠️ Key detail (NEW in 2024): Vikram was making a secret phone call at 8:45–9:00 PM. Phone records from 1987, recently digitised by BSNL archives, show a call from Iyer Haveli to Dr. Sujatha Reddy's clinic at 8:52 PM — lasting 11 minutes.

New Evidence
Insurance Policy
LIC of India · Policy № 48-7741-MYS

A ₹15 lakh life insurance policy taken out in 1979 — after Savithri's death. Beneficiary: originally Vikram Iyer. However, a beneficiary change form, dated October 10, 1987 (same day as the new will), was submitted to LIC naming the charitable trust.

⚠️ The form submission was never processed — it arrived at LIC offices on October 17, two days after the death. Under the original beneficiary designation, Vikram stood to collect ₹15 lakh. He did collect it in December 1987.

New Evidence
Dr. Reddy's Appointment Book · October 1987
Sourced from Reddy estate archive · 2023

Recovered from Dr. Sujatha Reddy's estate after her death in 2019. October 14, 1987 entry reads: "Iyer — 8 PM. V's instructions. Prepare dose. Back stairs if required."

⚠️ "V's instructions" — interpreted as Vikram Iyer. "Back stairs" — matches the unlatched window and the rear staircase described in the crime scene report. "Prepare dose" — the nature of this dose is unclear. Sleeping tonic, or poison?

Forensic Report
Post-Mortem Report
Dr. K. Venkataraman, Mysore Medical College · October 15, 1987

Cause of death: Acute arsenic poisoning. Arsenic trioxide detected in stomach, liver, and blood at levels of 4.2 mg/kg body weight — significantly above the lethal threshold of ~1–2 mg/kg.

Time of death: Estimated 10:30 PM – 12:30 AM based on body temperature, rigor mortis, and gastric contents.

Method: Ingested. Arsenic was dissolved in warm liquid — consistent with the milk glass. Onset of symptoms approximately 45–60 minutes after ingestion.

Other findings: No signs of external trauma. Mild phenobarbitol present — consistent with prior doses of sleeping medication. No defensive wounds. No signs of struggle.

2024 Update
Supplementary Forensic Analysis
CFSL Bengaluru · March 2024

Note re-analysis: The paper of the "suicide note" is sourced from a different ream than the study stationery. Ink: iron gall, consistent with 1987. However, spectral analysis reveals the ink was applied in a single sitting — not with the casual pauses typical of emotionally-composed writing. Conclusion: likely prewritten or copied from a template.

Thread analysis: Silk thread from window: Kanjivaram silk, maroon. Consistent with traditional South Indian formal wear. Microscopic examination shows the thread was snagged at waist height — consistent with someone reaching in to unlatch from outside.

Fingerprint re-analysis: Partial print on rat poison tin: not matched to any family member or household staff. Matches unknown. ⚠️ Possible match pending against Dr. Reddy's prints (sourced from 2019 death registration).
